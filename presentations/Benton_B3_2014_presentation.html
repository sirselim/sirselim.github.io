Epigenetics: what is it?

Epi-(GREEK:over,above) genetics

  • heritable changes in gene expression/phenotype
  • functionally relevant changes, doesn't modfiy underlying genomic sequence


Several mechanisms

  • Histone modifications (Acetylation and Methylation)
  • microRNA's
  • lncRNA's
  • DNA Methylation

DNA Methylation: a brief overview

Methylation can be thought of as punctuation for the genome:

"Woman without her man is nothing."

"Woman, without her man, is nothing."

"Woman! Without her, man is nothing."

DNA Methylation: a brief overview

  • 5'methylation of cytosine
    • typically at CpG dinucleotide
    • result is a reduction of gene expression (though there are exceptions)
  • Methylation is heritable - imprinting
  • BUT is also dynamic: response to environment & other stimuli

450k Array's: the technology

The Illumina Human 450K Methylation Chip:

  • Genome Wide: has >450,000 probes (individual CpG sites)
  • high-throughput and fast (12 samples per chip)
  • GRC within IHBI now have capability to run these
  • DNA is Bisulfite converted, fragmented and hybridised
  • difference in intensity between meth/unmeth probes gives \(\beta\).

\[\beta = \frac{meth}{(meth + unmeth + 100)}\]

  • values range between 0-1: can think of as percent methylation

Investigating Longitudinal Methylation Profiles: the design

Now we had this shiny new machine...

  • we needed a pilot study to:
    • put the Illumina HiScan through it's paces
    • help with training
    • hopefully provide some interesting data (!)

What we came up with:

  • 12 samples, healthy NI females
    • aged matched
    • never smoked, not on med's, healthy body metrics, etc.
  • two time-points, year 2000 and 2009/2010 follow-up
  • run across two Illumina 450k methylation chips

Investigating Longitudinal Methylation Profiles: the approach

  • Quality Control (removal of multiple-mapping and cross-reactive probes) saw a reduction in total CpGs to 442,298
  • R package glmnet
    • a binomal model with an elastic net was applied across all available CpGs (with time-point as bivariate)
citation('glmnet')

Jerome Friedman, Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani (2010). Regularization Paths for Generalized Linear Models via Coordinate Descent. Journal of Statistical Software, 33(1), 1-22. URL http://www.jstatsoft.org/v33/i01/.

  • PCA was used to visualise the separation of the individuals and variables across the top two components
    • (R package FactoMineR)
citation('FactoMineR')

Francois Husson, Julie Josse, Sebastien Le and Jeremy Mazet (2014). FactoMineR: Multivariate Exploratory Data Analysis and Data Mining with R. R package version 1.27. http://CRAN.R-project.org/package=FactoMineR

A few points of interest before moving forwards...

A quick summary thus far...


  • identified 147 CpG sites exhibiting differential methylation over ~10 years in the NI cohort
    • interestingly 95% were located in genes, with the majority of these in 'traditional' control regions
    • commonly see many more unannotated 'hits' (intergenic regions)
    • is not unusual to have ~25-40% of associated CpGs in unannotated regions


  • this potentially speaks to the importance of these genes and their regulation in response to specific (?) environmental stimuli

Methylation vs Phenotypic Traits Over Time

The Idea

  • investigate associations between change in (\(\Delta\)) methylation and phenotypic traits (measured over ~10 years)
  • there were 25 \(\Delta\beta\) (selected based on effect size)
    • > absolute 4% methylation change (hopefully can validate)
  • 23 \(\Delta\)traits were available between both NIHS data sets

Findings

  • identified 22 'significant' correlations (12 traits and 12 CpGs), see Table 1.
  • the top correlation was between delta glucose and delta cg06642177
    • Pearson's r = 0.84 (p-value = 0.001)

Serum and glucocorticoid-regulated kinase 1 (SGK1)

The top correlation noted above was with a probe located in the transcription start site of SGK1

Serum and glucocorticoid-regulated kinase 1, or SGK1, belongs to a family of serine/threonine kinases that is under acute transcriptional control by several stimuli, including serum and glucocorticoids. The kinase is activated by insulin and growth factors via phosphatidylinositide-3-kinase, phosphoinositide-dependent kinase PDK1 and mammalian target of rapamycin mTORC2.

  • seems biologically plausible and interesting:

Jeyaraj, S., Boehmer, C., Lang, F., & Palmada, M. (2007). Role of SGK1 kinase in regulating glucose transport via glucose transporter GLUT4. Biochemical and Biophysical Research Communications, 356(3), 629â€“35. doi:10.1016/j.bbrc.2007.03.029

  • SGK1 promotes glucose transporter membrane abundance via GLUT4 phosphorylation at Ser274
  • SGK1 may contribute to the insulin and GLUT4-dependent regulation of cellular glucose uptake

What about age you ask?


Good question!


  • additional modelling of age revealed a separate series of distinct markers, independent of those identified to change longitudinally


  • this finding suggests that specific methylation profiles are dynamically changing in response to various environmental stimuli independent of the effects of the ageing process
